Guns N' Roses

Guns N' Roses is a legendary American rock band known for their explosive stage presence, provocative lyrics, and a sound that blends hard rock, glam, and blues. With their debut album "Appetite for Destruction" (1987), they skyrocketed to global fame with hits like "Sweet Child o' Mine" and "Welcome to the Jungle", becoming iconic symbols of rock 'n' roll in the late 1980s and 1990s.

History

In March 1985, Axl Rose, Tracii Guns, Izzy Stradlin, Ole Beich, and Rob Gardner came together to form the band. The original members were from two different groups, L.A. Guns and Hollywood Rose. They merged the names of the two groups to form Guns N' Roses. After their formation, there were some member changes. Ole Beich left the group and was replaced by Duff McKagan. Tracii Guns was expelled from the band due to differences with Axl Rose, and he was replaced by Saul Hudson, known as Slash. Steven Adler also joined the band, completing the lineup with Axl, Slash, Izzy Stradlin, Duff, and Adler. This lineup of Axl, Slash, Izzy Stradlin, Duff, and Adler went on to achieve worldwide fame, making their debut at the Troubadour on June 6th.
